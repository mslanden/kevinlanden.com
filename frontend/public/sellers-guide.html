Preparing Your Property for Sale

Interior Preparation

  • Declutter and depersonalize all living spaces
  • Deep clean throughout, including carpets and windows
  • Make necessary repairs (leaking faucets, squeaky doors, etc.)
  • Update lighting and replace bulbs for brightness
  • Consider neutral paint for dated rooms
  • Stage key rooms to showcase functionality

Exterior & Curb Appeal

  • Maintain landscaping and clear overgrown areas
  • Power wash decks, patios, and exterior surfaces
  • Ensure driveway and access roads are well-maintained
  • Highlight views and outdoor living spaces
  • Address any deferred maintenance issues
  • Create welcoming entry areas

Rural Property Specific Items

  • Service septic system and provide recent pumping records
  • Test well water and provide quality reports
  • Organize documentation for water rights and easements
  • Ensure generator and backup systems are operational
  • Clear defensible space per fire safety requirements
  • Document any renewable energy systems

Understanding the Current Market

Mountain and rural property markets have unique characteristics:

  • Seasonal Patterns: Peak activity often in spring and early fall
  • Buyer Demographics: Often relocating from urban areas or seeking recreational properties
  • Financing Considerations: Some buyers may need rural-specific loan programs
  • Inspection Periods: Longer due to well, septic, and property surveys
  • Closing Timelines: May be extended for rural-specific items
